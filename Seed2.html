THE SEED

Part 2 · Agbadarigi Settlements, Oyo Kingdom · the caves of ecstasy

We are specks in a kingdom of giants, beneath trees so vast that five men could not encircle their girth. Some rise so high that the vines coiling around them seem like slender trees, dark and muscular, reaching upward in mimicry. Here, everything breathes on a grander scale. And yet we stain this paradise — our sweat, our blood, our waste seeping into its roots. Every camp we raise and every tree we fell is a desecration, a small scar on something holy.

Fish scatter as I wade through streams so clear they seem made of glass. Pebbles roll beneath my feet, smooth and cold as bone. The air shifts; a wind howls through the valley, clawing at my cloak and stinging my face with sand. It crashes against the cliffs ahead with the violence of a hammer striking an anvil, its echo returning warped and alive, a creature of noise and hunger.

Ajasin moves beside me, his voice cutting through the wind's snarl. "We are close." His eyes catch the faint light, sharp and luminous, like lightning caught in human form. He leads the strike band — twenty men now, swelling with each ridge we cross. With every recruit, the air thickens, the world vibrating with a low, electric hum, as though the heavens themselves are grinding their teeth, building toward a storm.

Somewhere below, in the throats of the valleys, the earth moans — a strange harmony of sighs and cries. It rises and falls in waves, a chorus of ecstasy and agony, the living music of the caves where raw emotion sleeps and dreams.

"If we go any further, the seed of lust will overwhelm you," Ajasin says. "Can you feel anything?"

I close my eyes and open the windows of my mind. A faint charge of arousal caresses me. "Yes."

"Now concentrate and find the one soul different from the rest."

I try to keep my eyes on the billowing smoke from the warrior-priest camps, but the air in the valley thickens like honey. With the help of Ajasin, I lower myself to the cooling grass as the first wave of ecstasy gathers behind my ribs.

What touches me is not skin but something deeper — still felt in the body, still burning through nerves. The boundaries of pleasure and breath self unite.

Desire seizes, pulls into its tide. Carries beyond the limits of flesh in breath and form.

The sensation fractures into images —

The low moan of the earth beneath pulses, shifting cadence and mimicking the kpan-kpan-kpan of a village drum. The jagged hills dissolve, replaced by the ghost-shimmer of cassava leaves in the dusk.

No. You must stop this. Focus.

I wake to hills shimmering in a blaze of light that splits through clouds.

A force numbs me. My body arches.

My skull is a kiln that bakes and cracks my thoughts.

Heat.

I am dragged back down.

My mind flails between ecstasy and guilt — pleasure laced with something forbidden, something that should belong only to Shola.

The hilly skyline tilts and crackles in a fire, stars spill like millet from a gourd, all of it a fever-dream of earth and sky coalescing into the drum's throat — kpan-kpan-kpan.

The fire in my skull cools. A sharp sting blooms across my face. The tang of smoke and grass replaces the scent of millet.

✦ ✦ ✦

My cheek throbs.

"Wake up."

I open my eyes.

Ajasin looms over me.

I am sprawled on the grass, his palm still burning on my cheek. Beside us, the earth falls away into a dusk-soaked valley, hemmed in by jagged hills. From their hollows, sound rises — low, rhythmic, unbroken.

Memory returns slowly.

We reached this place not long ago — the den of the Seed. The Afefe warrior-priests stood around me. I reached out with my mind, searching for the Seed's pulse —

— and lost myself.

The pleasure struck like lightning, tore through me until I broke open.

"Did you find the Seed?" Ajasin asks.

I smile. My lips feel strange, stretched. "It was amazing."

"What?"

"Being burned alive in ecstasy."

"Did you find the ajogun?"

"I couldn't." I can't stop smiling.

He presses an armband into my hand. The leather is warm, worn smooth, threaded with small charms that clink faintly. "Take this. It will shield you from its possession."

I fasten it below my shoulder muscle, the strap biting into my skin.

"Remember your training. Go back. Focus this time."

I close my eyes.

Around us, the caves throb — sound swelling and collapsing like a lung too large for its ribs. It seeps into bone, into thought, into the soft places behind the eyes. Part of me leans toward it, a tired animal craving a mate.

I hold myself.

And reach again.

Something brushes my mind — quick, unwelcome, like fingers rifling through hay. A silent rhythm hums beneath, shared, seamless.

All but one.

There —

a ripple, thin as a crack in glass.

Then a taste follows.

Not imagined —

felt.

Thick.

Oily.

Smoke curls across my tongue. Char clings to the back of my throat.

Fish.

Someone apart. Eating. Separate.

I pull back as if burned and open my eyes.

✦ ✦ ✦

Ajasin and Adeniki stand where I left them, watching me like men watching a door that might explode.

"I can take you to him," I say, my voice still carrying the echo of shared pulses.

Badimun doesn't raise his voice. "Protect them, and you join them."

"I am not afraid of death."

Adeniki turns, urgency breaking through his composure. "There is no time."

Badimun hesitates. It is small, but I see it — the tightening at the corners of his eyes, the shift of his weight. Then he nods once. "I will speak to him."

He turns and disappears into the dark, his form swallowed by the cave mouth where the air pulses in and out like breath.

The caves breathe around us.

Warm air rolls out in slow waves, carrying the smell of sweat, damp stone, and something sweet that has begun to sour. The sound presses closer now, thicker, layered — voices slipping over one another until they lose shape.

Ajasin's glare never leaves me. His chest rises and falls, controlled, but his fingers flex at his sides as if they remember the knife he almost drew.

Moments later, Badimun returns. His face is set, unreadable.

"We accept."

"Swear it," I say. "No harm comes to me or them."

"We give our word."

"Take off your armbands."

Adeniki blinks. "What?"

"All of you."

Reluctance flickers across their faces — quick, sharp. Then, one by one, they comply. Leather creaks. Knots loosen. Charms slide free. The armbands fall to stone with dull, final thuds that echo too loudly in the confined space.

Ajasin is last.

His fingers linger on his band, pressing into it as though it anchors him. His jaw tightens. Then he rips it free and drops it. It lands harder than the rest.

I gather them. Their weight is wrong — heavier than leather should be, as if they hold something inside them.

"Only you and Adeniki come with me," I say to Badimun. "The rest stay."

Ajasin's voice cuts through, sharp. "Hurry. The sound is fading."

I hand two armbands back, sling the rest over my shoulder, and turn toward the descent.

"Only two of you follow," I add without looking back. "The rest wait here. With Ajasin."

The caves breathe heat.

Torchlight shivers along wet stone, the flames bending and stretching as currents of air pass through unseen channels. Moisture clings to the walls, gathering in beads that catch the light before slipping down in thin trails.

Bodies move in the half-light — slick with sweat, glistening bronze and shadow. They slide past one another, merging, separating, limbs tangling and unwinding without pause. There is no beginning, no end — only motion.

The air is thick.

Humid with breath.

Heavy with salt and musk and something sweeter beneath it — overripe, turning at the edges.

Badimun, Adeniki, and I push through. Skin brushes skin. Hands reach without seeing. Eyes half-lidded, unfocused.

At a rise, I stop.

Below —

a mass.

Limbs layered over limbs, backs arching, heads thrown back, mouths open. The sound gathers there, dense and vibrating, until it becomes something else entirely — something that hums behind the eyes and presses at the skull.

My body answers before I can stop it.

A tightening. A pull.

Then —

a fracture.

A hitch in the rhythm. A voice breaking where it should not.

A flicker of something wrong.

Shame.

Thought.

The spell is thinning.

Without that seamless unity, the Seed will hide better. The current breaks. The signal weakens.

I move again, forcing my limbs forward, cutting through passages where the walls narrow and the air grows hotter. My shoulder scrapes stone. My fingers slide against damp rock as I steady myself.

I follow that difference.

Faint.

Stubborn.

Through a narrow opening, I climb. The stone is slick, ridged with mineral veins that bite into my palms. My foot slips once, sending a scatter of grit down into the dark below. I pull myself up.

At the crown of a hill, a crowd gathers, their bodies packed close, their voices rising in a low, broken moan that trembles unevenly.

Badimun and Adeniki follow, breath steady, eyes sharp.

A woman rushes past.

Bare.

Her skin gleams with sweat, her chest heaving. She clutches her clothes to her chest as though they might hold her together, the fabric twisted tight in her fists.

"Abike... what have you done..." Her voice frays, each word unraveling into breath.

She reaches the edge.

Looks down.

The drop yawns beneath her — a dark mouth lined with jagged stone.

Don't.

She steps forward.

Gone.

The sound of her body striking below does not reach us.

Badimun and Adeniki rush to the edge, peering over. Their silhouettes cut against the dim glow, rigid, searching.

I stay where I am.

I do not want to see her below — to see what remains, to feel the pull to mend what cannot be mended.

This is not meant to happen.

"Are you satisfied?" Badimun says, not turning. "That soul is lost because of you."

I don't answer.

Across the hill —

I see him.

Firelight licks upward, thin flames snapping and bending. Smoke curls into the dark, carrying the sharp, greasy scent of roasting flesh.

Fish.

At the center, he sits.

He's young — late teen or older. His shoulders are narrow, his posture slack, as though he is only half-present in his own body.

The others lean toward him, drawn without knowing why.

"That is her," I say, pointing at the woman beside him.

Badimun and Adeniki don't hesitate. They descend, their movements controlled, precise — feet finding holds without looking, bodies flowing over the uneven ground. They cross the valley and climb the opposing hill with practiced ease.

The others laugh when they seize her.

The sound is wrong — too bright, too sharp.

She fights, screams, claws at the air, her fingers raking across their arms. But her friends laugh harder, their faces open, uncomprehending.

"Are you tired of giving yourself?" one says, grinning. "Be happy. These fine men want you."

Her voice tears as Badimun and Adeniki carry her off into the valley.

It fades.

Swallowed.

Time to move.

I climb, palms scraping against stone. Skin splits. Pain bites — sharp, clean, anchoring.

At the top, six figures circle the fire.

And at the center —

him.

The Seed.

The others call him Abayomi.

He has a weak jaw and the heavy eyelids of a tortoise. His lips are slightly parted, his breath slow, almost bored. Nothing in his face inspires desire. Nothing commands attention — not even his voice.

Yet when he speaks, everyone stops.

They turn.

They listen.

They obey.

The wind stirs.

It rises, rushing through the cave mouths, carrying distant voices. It roars — then fades.

The Afefe are returning.

I must hurry.

I step into the circle.

No one notices.

I wait.

I reach out.

My fingers brush his arm.

A spark —

sharp,

electric —

He jerks, eyes snapping open. "Who touched me?"

The wind surges. Voices rise. The fire gutters, shadows tearing loose from the walls, stretching and snapping.

I don't have time.

I throw myself at him, wrap my arms around him, and hold tight.

The connection slams into place.

Emotion floods —

fear,

confusion,

hunger —

then something deeper.

Not him.

Something within him.

An indwelling.

It stirs.

Rises.

Breaks —

and pours through me like a breached dam.

It pries me open from the inside, folding my thoughts into something vast and faceless.

Shaping into that which is not mine.

Stretching.

Replicating.

Ecstasy.

My body is there, but distant — arms locked, fingers digging into flesh that no longer feels separate from mine.

I cannot tell where I begin or end.

It hollows and fills like an air sac, expanding and collapsing without rhythm.

Light returns slowly.

Reluctantly.

Two faces hover above — Badimun and Adeniki. Their outlines waver, as though seen through heat.

"You lied," Adeniki says. Her voice is flat, but her eyes are not.

I blink. The world lurches, struggling to hold shape. "How did you know?"

"No one fought to prevent her capture," she replies.

I push myself upright. The ground tilts. My hand presses into dirt, damp and cold. "There. That is the Seed." I point.

Abayomi scrambles nearby, fumbling with his clothes, his fingers clumsy, his eyes wide and unfocused.

Chaos erupts.

Badimun and Adeniki move.

The others follow.

He runs — stumbles — catches himself — then vanishes into shadow, slipping between bodies, into darkness.

He must have said something.

Because his followers turn.

They pick up rocks — jagged, heavy — and begin to throw them. The stones whistle through the air, striking ground, flesh, stone.

"Find him!" Badimun snaps at me. "This is your fault."

The link —

it's still there.

A thread.

Thin.

Taut.

Trembling.

"Knock me out," I say.

Adeniki stares. "What?"

"Do it."

He hesitates.

A fist flashes.

Impact —

There is no pain.

Only the knowledge of it.

The sensation slips through me, travels the thread —

finds him.

The ground lurches.

I fall.

The link snaps.

My feelings rush back in — jagged, raw, too sharp.

I drag in a breath. It burns.

Force myself up.

"Where is he?" Adeniki demands.

I point toward a valley where shadow pools thick and unmoving. "Down there. He hit his head. He's out."

They don't question it.

They plunge into the dark.

On the ridge, Abayomi's followers gather, peering down but unwilling to descend. Their bodies press together, uncertain now.

"Abayomi!" they call.

The name breaks in their mouths.

Some weep. Tears cut pale tracks through the grime on their faces.

Badimun and Adeniki do not return.

I lie back.

The stone beneath me is still warm.

I stare into the black above, where the cave ceiling swallows what little light remains.

The cave presses close.

I wake to quiet.

No movement.

No sound.

The bodies are gone.

The caves feel... empty. Hollowed.

Even the air has changed — cooler, thinner, as though something has been taken with it.

✦ ✦ ✦

Inside me —

something shifts.

A looseness.

Alive.

Writhing.

Not foreign.

Not separate.

But one with my memories.

The Seed's indwelling stores and unleashes the spirit of lust.

I absorbed and copied it.

The power of ecstasy is mine to unleash on whomever I choose.

✦ ✦ ✦

What they sought to bury

has taken root in me.

What they fear,

I have become.

What repulses them,

I revere.

One man's curse,

another man's deliverance.

I smile weakly.

But all I want now —

is Shola.
